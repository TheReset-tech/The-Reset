· Why willpower fails ·

The science most programs ignore

01
The brain builds highways

Every repeated behaviour strengthens a neural pathway. After years of use, the pattern is faster and more powerful than conscious thought. You can't outthink it.

02
Willpower is the wrong tool

Willpower uses the prefrontal cortex — the brain's newest, most exhaustible system. It was never designed to override deep behavioural loops. That's why it always runs out.

03
Triggers are the real lock

Every relapse follows a trigger — a specific emotional state, environment, or time of day. Most people never map their triggers. We map yours in week one.

04
Identity is the key

Lasting change doesn't come from stopping a behaviour. It comes from becoming someone who no longer needs it. The Reset is an identity program, not a habit tracker.

· Common questions ·

FAQ

Q Is this completely confidential?

Yes — completely. Everything you share in your questionnaire, on your call, and throughout the program is strictly confidential. We don't use real names in any case studies without explicit written consent. Your Telegram handle is the only identity we ever see.

Q How is this different from NoFap or other programs?

NoFap is a community built on willpower and streaks. It helps some people — but it has no mechanism for understanding why you relapse or what specifically triggers you. The Reset is a personalised behavioral science program. We map your specific pattern and build tools around it. It's the difference between a running club and a physiotherapist.

Q What if I relapse during the program?

We expect it — especially in the first two weeks. A relapse is data, not failure. Every relapse tells us something specific about your trigger pattern that we use to refine your framework. The goal is not a perfect streak. The goal is understanding your pattern so thoroughly that relapses become rarer and shorter until they stop entirely.

Q What does the free Chapter 1 include?

Chapter 1 is the trigger mapping exercise — the exact framework we use in week one of the program. You can begin working through it before your call, which means your first session goes deeper faster. It's yours to keep regardless of whether you join the full program.
